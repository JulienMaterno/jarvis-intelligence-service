## Part 3: The Algenie Story

### How It Started: The "Wrong Turn" to Australia

Aaron was supposed to go to Vietnam for his German thesis at a startup. Instead, through a spreadsheet mishap, he ended up interviewing with an Australian fish farming startup (Aquacultr) that he mistakenly thought was in Vietnam.

**The Psychedelic Fish Speaker Incident:**

On his first day at Aquacultr, the CEO revealed he had taken mushrooms two weeks prior. During his trip, he "talked to water" and learned that "fish would like some music." The entire business model was pivoting to build underwater speakers for fish.

After five days of investigation, Aaron quit. The "research" showed:
1. No scientific evidence for music calming fish
2. Volume requirements would damage fish hearing
3. No way to measure if fish are actually calmed

This left Aaron stranded in Newcastle, Australia, with a lease and no thesis project.

### Finding Algenie

After breaking his lease and moving to Sydney with a deadline of end-of-October, Aaron found Algenie through LinkedIn outreach. He met the founders (Nick and John) at a cafÃ©, and they said: "You can start now."

Instead of leaving, Aaron chose to stay because he genuinely enjoyed the work and believed in the mission.
